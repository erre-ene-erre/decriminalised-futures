Textcontent: <p><em>LONS was a two part course that ran for three months between October to December 2021, and again between April to June 2022. Each month we collectively explored a new topic. The focus was less on current legislations and campaigns, and more at how we got where we are today.</em></p><p><em>This is an archive of each session in the series. Here you will find recordings, reading lists, audio, and summaries of each section, as well as details of our hosts and where you can find them now.</em></p><p></p><h2>Sex Worker Art and Creative Practices (June 2022)</h2><p>This session looked at sex worker art and activism worldwide to learn about the role creativity has played in our struggles for change.</p><p><em>Lecture</em> by Yves Sanglante, hosted by Tamara-Jade Kaz (Monday June 6th 7pm – 8.30pm)</p><p><em>Seminar</em> hosted by Tamara-Jade Kaz (Monday June 13th 6.30pm – 8.30pm)</p><p></p><p><strong><em>Yves Sanglante (they/them) </em></strong>is an artist, performer and curator interested in art as a means to inspire liberation. Through their work, they explore overlooked and forgotten stories from queer, trans, and sex worker communities to understand the present, and inform visions of the future. In 2016, as part of a collective of sex workers, artists and anthropologists, Sanglante co-founded sex worker-led archive <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230601222847/https://www.projectofdesire.co.uk/" target="_blank"><em>‘Objects of Desire’</em></a>. They also co-curated the Decriminalised Futures exhibition which is currently showing at Institute of Contemporary Arts, London. You can follow their work at <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230601222847/https://www.instagram.com/nostalgia.nausea/" target="_blank"><em>‘Nostalgia Nausea’</em></a>.</p><p><strong><em>Tamara-Jade Kaz</em></strong> is an artist, activist and educator based in east London. Her art practice explores themes of marginalised identity, social justice and community, primarily through illustration. She has seven years of experience as a trainer and facilitator for social change groups. she specialises in developing bespoke programmes and workshops that challenge groups to reflect on strategy, structural power, group culture and relationships.She combines her passion for facilitation and visual art through her practice as a <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230601222847/https://www.tamarajadekaz.co.uk/visual-notetaking" target="_blank"><em>visual notetaker</em></a>. Tamara-Jade’s background also includes working in and organising around gendered violence prevention from a Black feminist perspective. An intersectional analysis of these issues is particularly important to her as a Black lesbian. In 2019, Tamara-Jade was appointed to the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230601222847/http://www.goodnightoutcampaign.org/" target="_blank"><em>good night out campaign</em></a> board of directors; a CIC that works with nightlife spaces and organisations to better understand, respond to, and prevent sexual harassment and assault. She is a member of the <a href="https://web.archive.org/web/20230601222847/https://www.uvwunion.org.uk/en/sectors/designers-cultural-workers/" target="_blank"><em>designers and cultural workers</em></a> branch of United Voices of the World trade union, and the association of illustrators.</p><p></p><p><em><sub>This course is made possible with generous support from the Barry Amiel &amp; Norman Melburn Trust and the Rosa Luxemburg Foundation.</sub></em></p><p></p>
